Overlay Design Pattern Research

April 23, 2026

As a user, I find it fairly annoying when I’m simply reading an article or a comic and these alerts constantly overshadow my main view. As a designer, however, I now understand why these alerts are so persistent after reading“Best Practices for Modals, Overlays, and Dialog Windows” by Nick Babich. From the article, I learned that modals are commonly used to grab the user’s attention during a task, especially when displaying critical information such as warnings, errors, or requests for essential input, as well as guiding users through short, focused interactions.

However, the article emphasizes the importance of avoiding overuse. Modals should be kept short and simple so they don’t overwhelm the user. Designers can also help users stay focused by using a dark overlay to highlight the modal. To prevent frustration caused by persistence, it’s important to provide multiple ways to exit the modal, such as a close (“X”) button, a cancel button, the escape key, or even allowing users to click outside the modal to dismiss it.

By following these design strategies, we can reduce user frustration and make modals feel more intentional and necessary, rather than repetitive or intrusive.
